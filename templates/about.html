About the FLOW Dataset

This app is currently built around the FLOW project dataset , a citizen science study on freshwater stream monitoring in Germany. The FLOW project engaged community members in monitoring local streams, collecting ecological data alongside professional researchers. Participants completed surveys before joining the project, immediately after, and at a follow-up several months later. This pre/post/follow-up design allows us to measure whether and how participation changed people's knowledge, attitudes, and behaviour.

The dataset includes responses from three groups: Citizen Scientists who actively participated in stream monitoring, a Waiting Control group who were registered but had not yet participated, and a Panel Control group with no involvement in the project. Comparing these groups allows us to assess whether changes are genuinely due to citizen science participation or simply reflect broader trends over time.

What you can explore

Choose from 14 psychological and behavioural metrics, including knowledge scores, environmental attitudes and planned behaviour. For each metric, you will see:

  • All individual data points across timepoints
  • A comparison between Citizen Scientists, Waiting Control, and Panel Control groups
  • Statistical significance markers showing where real change occurred
  • A description of what each metric measures

Statistical approach

Significance testing is performed using the Wilcoxon signed-rank test, a non-parametric test chosen because the score distributions are not guaranteed to be normally distributed, and because the data is paired (the same participants responded at each timepoint). Significance markers follow standard conventions: * p ≤ 0.05, ** p ≤ 0.01, *** p ≤ 0.001, no marker = not significant.
