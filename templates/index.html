Citizen Science Visualizer

Citizen science brings together researchers and communities to tackle real scientific questions. But what does participation actually do to the people involved? Does joining a citizen science project change how much someone knows about a topic? Does it shift attitudes, behaviour, or sense of personal responsibility?

The Citizen Science Visualizer is an open, interactive tool that makes these questions visible. It lets you explore survey data collected from participants before, after, and at follow-up, comparing how different groups changed over time across a range of psychological and behavioural metrics.

Who is the this for?

Whether you are a researcher evaluating the impact of a citizen science programme, a science communicator looking for clear ways to present results, or a citizen scientist curious about the broader effects of your own participation, this tool is for you.

Why does it matter?

There are very few accessible tools for visualizing the human impact of citizen science. Most results stay locked in academic papers. This visualizer is a step toward changing that, making evidence for citizen science impact open, visual, and understandable to everyone.
